Core Concepts

Understanding the hierarchy of hazard controls is essential for any safety program.

[ Hierarchy of Controls Diagram ]
Figure 1 — The five levels of hazard control, from most to least effective.

The hierarchy of controls is a system used to minimize or eliminate exposure to hazards. It prioritizes actions from most effective (elimination) to least effective (PPE). Employers are required by law to work through the hierarchy before relying on personal protective equipment alone.

⚡ Key Point Elimination and substitution are the most effective controls because they remove the hazard entirely, rather than just protecting workers from it.
